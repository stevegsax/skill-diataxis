# Fraction Word Problems

In this tutorial, we will solve real-world problems that involve fractions. We will practice identifying which operation to use, translating words into fraction expressions, and interpreting results in context. By the end, you will be able to approach any fraction word problem with a clear method.

## What we need before starting

You should be comfortable with all four fraction operations. See the tutorials on [Adding and Subtracting](adding-subtracting-fractions.md) and [Multiplying and Dividing](multiplying-dividing-fractions.md) if you need a refresher.

## Step 1: Identify the operation

Before computing anything, ask: what is the problem asking me to do?

- **Combining parts?** Addition.
- **Finding what's left?** Subtraction.
- **"Of" or scaling?** Multiplication.
- **Splitting or fitting?** Division.

Let's work through four problems, one for each pattern.

## Step 2: An addition problem

> You drink $\frac{1}{3}$ of a bottle of water in the morning and $\frac{2}{5}$ in the afternoon. What fraction of the bottle have you consumed?

We are combining two parts, so we add:

$$\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{5}$$

Find the LCD of $3$ and $5$: it is $15$.

$$\frac{1}{3} = \frac{5}{15}, \quad \frac{2}{5} = \frac{6}{15}$$

$$\frac{5}{15} + \frac{6}{15} = \frac{11}{15}$$

**Answer**: You have consumed $\frac{11}{15}$ of the bottle.

**Check**: $\frac{11}{15} \approx 0.733$, which is less than $1$. That makes sense — you have not yet finished the bottle.

## Step 3: A subtraction problem

> A board is $\frac{7}{8}$ of a meter long. You cut off $\frac{1}{4}$ of a meter. How long is the remaining piece?

We are finding what's left, so we subtract:

$$\frac{7}{8} - \frac{1}{4}$$

The LCD of $8$ and $4$ is $8$:

$$\frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{8}$$

$$\frac{7}{8} - \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8}$$

**Answer**: The remaining piece is $\frac{5}{8}$ of a meter.

**Check**: $0.875 - 0.25 = 0.625 = \frac{5}{8}$. Correct.

## Step 4: A multiplication problem (recipe scaling)

> A recipe calls for $\frac{3}{4}$ cup of flour. You want to make $\frac{2}{3}$ of the recipe. How much flour do you need?

The word "of" signals multiplication:

$$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{2 \times 3}{3 \times 4} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$$

**Answer**: You need $\frac{1}{2}$ cup of flour.

**Check**: $0.667 \times 0.75 = 0.5 = \frac{1}{2}$. Correct.

## Step 5: A division problem

> You have $3\frac{3}{4}$ meters of ribbon. Each gift bow requires $\frac{5}{8}$ of a meter. How many bows can you make?

We are asking "how many times does $\frac{5}{8}$ fit into $3\frac{3}{4}$?" — this is division.

Convert: $3\frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{4}$.

$$\frac{15}{4} \div \frac{5}{8} = \frac{15}{4} \times \frac{8}{5} = \frac{120}{20} = 6$$

**Answer**: You can make $6$ bows.

**Check**: $6 \times \frac{5}{8} = \frac{30}{8} = \frac{15}{4} = 3\frac{3}{4}$. Correct.

## What we accomplished

You have learned to:

- Identify which fraction operation a word problem requires
- Translate the problem into a fraction expression
- Solve and interpret the answer in context
- Verify the result with a quick check
