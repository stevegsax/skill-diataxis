# How to Scale a Recipe Using Fractions

This guide shows you how to adjust ingredient quantities when making a larger or smaller batch of a recipe.

## Prerequisites

You can multiply fractions and mixed numbers.

## Steps

1. **Determine the scaling factor.** Divide the desired servings by the original servings.

    Original: $8$ servings. Desired: $6$ servings.

    $$\text{Scaling factor} = \frac{6}{8} = \frac{3}{4}$$

2. **Multiply each ingredient quantity by the scaling factor.**

    Original: $2\frac{1}{2}$ cups of flour.

    $$2\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{8} = 1\frac{7}{8} \text{ cups}$$

3. **Repeat for every ingredient.**

    Original: $\frac{2}{3}$ cup sugar.

    $$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2} \text{ cup}$$

4. **Round to practical measurements if needed.** If a result like $\frac{7}{8}$ cup is hard to measure, round to the nearest measurable quantity (e.g., $1$ cup minus $2$ tablespoons, since $1$ tablespoon $= \frac{1}{16}$ cup).

## Scaling up

For doubling, the scaling factor is $\frac{2}{1} = 2$. For tripling, $\frac{3}{1} = 3$.

$$\frac{3}{4} \text{ cup} \times 2 = \frac{3}{2} = 1\frac{1}{2} \text{ cups}$$
