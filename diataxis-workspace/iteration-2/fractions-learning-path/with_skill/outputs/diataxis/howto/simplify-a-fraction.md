# How to Simplify a Fraction

This guide shows you how to reduce any fraction to its lowest terms.

## Prerequisites

You can identify the numerator and denominator of a fraction and are familiar with finding factors of a number.

## Steps

1. **Identify the numerator and denominator.** For $\frac{18}{24}$, the numerator is $18$ and the denominator is $24$.

2. **Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of the numerator and denominator.** List factors or use prime factorization:

    - Factors of $18$: $1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18$
    - Factors of $24$: $1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24$
    - GCF $= 6$

3. **Divide both numerator and denominator by the GCF.**

    $$\frac{18}{24} = \frac{18 \div 6}{24 \div 6} = \frac{3}{4}$$

4. **Verify the result is in lowest terms.** Confirm that $3$ and $4$ share no common factors other than $1$.

## Edge cases

- **Already simplified**: If the GCF is $1$, the fraction is already in lowest terms. No further work needed.
- **Whole number result**: If the numerator is a multiple of the denominator, simplify to a whole number: $\frac{12}{4} = 3$.
- **Zero numerator**: $\frac{0}{n} = 0$ for any nonzero $n$. Already simplified.
