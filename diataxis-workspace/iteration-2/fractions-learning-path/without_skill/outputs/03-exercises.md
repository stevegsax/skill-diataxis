# Practice Exercises

Work through these exercises with pencil and paper. Answers are in the next file (04-answer-key.md). Resist the urge to peek until you have attempted each problem.

## Set A: Fundamentals

1. Simplify 24/36.
2. Simplify 45/60.
3. Convert 23/5 to a mixed number.
4. Convert 7 3/8 to an improper fraction.
5. List three fractions equivalent to 2/7.
6. Which is larger: 4/9 or 5/11?

## Set B: Addition and Subtraction

7. 3/8 + 5/8
8. 7/10 - 3/10
9. 1/6 + 3/8
10. 5/9 + 7/12
11. 7/8 - 2/3
12. 4 2/5 + 3 1/3
13. 6 1/4 - 2 5/6
14. 3/7 + 2/5 - 1/2

## Set C: Multiplication and Division

15. 3/4 x 2/5
16. 7/9 x 3/14
17. 5/6 x 12/25
18. 2 1/3 x 1 4/5
19. 5/8 / 3/4
20. 9/10 / 3/5
21. 4 1/2 / 2 1/4
22. 7/12 / 7/6

## Set D: Mixed Operations and Order of Operations

23. 1/2 + 1/3 x 3/4
24. (2/3 + 1/6) x 4/5
25. 5/8 - 1/4 / 2
26. (3/4 - 1/3) / (1/2 + 1/6)
27. 2/3 x 3/4 + 1/6 x 2/3

## Set E: Conversions

28. Convert 7/8 to a decimal.
29. Convert 5/11 to a decimal (round to three decimal places).
30. Convert 0.375 to a fraction in lowest terms.
31. Convert 0.8333... (repeating 3) to a fraction.
32. Arrange from least to greatest: 2/5, 3/7, 4/9

## Set F: Word Problems

33. A board is 3 3/4 feet long. You cut off a piece that is 1 2/3 feet. How long is the remaining piece?

34. A tank is 2/5 full. After adding 15 gallons, the tank is 7/10 full. What is the total capacity of the tank?

35. Three friends split a restaurant bill. The bill is $84. One person pays 1/3, another pays 3/7. What fraction does the third person pay, and how much is that in dollars?

36. You run 2 1/4 miles on Monday, 1 5/8 miles on Wednesday, and 3 1/2 miles on Friday. What is your total mileage for the week?

37. A recipe serves 4 people and calls for 3/4 cup of sugar. How much sugar do you need to serve 10 people?

38. A pipe fills 1/6 of a pool per hour. A second pipe fills 1/4 of the pool per hour. Working together, what fraction of the pool do they fill in one hour? How many hours to fill the entire pool?
