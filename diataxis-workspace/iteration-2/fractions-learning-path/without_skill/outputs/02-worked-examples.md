# Worked Examples

Step-by-step solutions to representative problems. Study these before attempting the exercises.

## Example 1: Simplifying a Fraction

**Problem:** Simplify 42/56.

**Solution:**

1. Find the GCD of 42 and 56.
    - 42 = 2 x 3 x 7
    - 56 = 2 x 2 x 2 x 7
    - Common prime factors: 2 and 7 --> GCD = 14
2. Divide both by 14.
    - 42 / 14 = 3
    - 56 / 14 = 4
3. **Answer: 3/4**

## Example 2: Adding Fractions with Unlike Denominators

**Problem:** 2/9 + 5/12

**Solution:**

1. Find the LCD of 9 and 12.
    - Multiples of 9: 9, 18, 27, 36, ...
    - Multiples of 12: 12, 24, 36, ...
    - LCD = 36
2. Convert each fraction.
    - 2/9 = (2 x 4) / (9 x 4) = 8/36
    - 5/12 = (5 x 3) / (12 x 3) = 15/36
3. Add the numerators.
    - 8/36 + 15/36 = 23/36
4. Check: 23 and 36 share no common factors.
5. **Answer: 23/36**

## Example 3: Subtracting Mixed Numbers

**Problem:** 5 1/3 - 2 3/4

**Solution:**

1. Convert to improper fractions.
    - 5 1/3 = (5 x 3 + 1) / 3 = 16/3
    - 2 3/4 = (2 x 4 + 3) / 4 = 11/4
2. Find the LCD of 3 and 4 = 12.
    - 16/3 = 64/12
    - 11/4 = 33/12
3. Subtract.
    - 64/12 - 33/12 = 31/12
4. Convert back to a mixed number.
    - 31 / 12 = 2 remainder 7
    - 31/12 = 2 7/12
5. **Answer: 2 7/12**

## Example 4: Multiplying Fractions with Cross-Cancellation

**Problem:** 15/28 x 14/25

**Solution:**

1. Before multiplying, look for common factors across numerators and denominators.
    - 15 and 25 share a factor of 5: 15/5 = 3, 25/5 = 5
    - 14 and 28 share a factor of 14: 14/14 = 1, 28/14 = 2
2. After cancellation: 3/2 x 1/5 = 3/10
3. **Answer: 3/10**

## Example 5: Dividing Fractions

**Problem:** 7/8 / 3/4

**Solution:**

1. Keep the first fraction: 7/8
2. Change division to multiplication.
3. Flip the second fraction: 3/4 becomes 4/3.
4. Multiply: 7/8 x 4/3
5. Cross-cancel: 4 and 8 share a factor of 4 --> 1/2 x 1/3... wait, let's be precise:
    - 7/8 x 4/3 = 7/(8) x 4/3
    - 8 and 4 share factor 4: 8/4 = 2, 4/4 = 1
    - Result: 7/2 x 1/3 = 7/6
6. Convert: 7/6 = 1 1/6
7. **Answer: 7/6 or 1 1/6**

## Example 6: Ordering Fractions

**Problem:** Arrange from least to greatest: 3/8, 5/12, 1/3

**Solution:**

1. Find the LCD of 8, 12, and 3 = 24.
2. Convert each fraction.
    - 3/8 = 9/24
    - 5/12 = 10/24
    - 1/3 = 8/24
3. Order by numerator: 8/24, 9/24, 10/24
4. **Answer: 1/3, 3/8, 5/12**

## Example 7: Converting a Repeating Decimal to a Fraction

**Problem:** Convert 0.666... to a fraction.

**Solution:**

1. Let x = 0.666...
2. Multiply both sides by 10: 10x = 6.666...
3. Subtract the original: 10x - x = 6.666... - 0.666...
4. 9x = 6
5. x = 6/9 = 2/3
6. **Answer: 2/3**

## Example 8: Word Problem

**Problem:** A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of flour. You want to make 1 1/2 times the recipe. How much flour do you need?

**Solution:**

1. Convert the multiplier: 1 1/2 = 3/2
2. Multiply: 2/3 x 3/2
3. Cross-cancel the 3s: 2/1 x 1/2 = 2/2 = 1
4. **Answer: 1 cup of flour**
