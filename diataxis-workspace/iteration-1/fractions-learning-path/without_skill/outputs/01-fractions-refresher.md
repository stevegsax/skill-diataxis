# Fractions Refresher

This guide covers fractions from the ground up, written for adults who learned this
material before and want to brush up. It moves quickly through the basics and spends
more time on the operations and techniques that tend to get rusty.

## What a Fraction Is

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It has two components:

- **Numerator** (top number) -- how many parts you have
- **Denominator** (bottom number) -- how many equal parts make up the whole

For example, 3/4 means "3 out of 4 equal parts."

### Types of Fractions

- **Proper fraction** -- numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g. 3/8)
- **Improper fraction** -- numerator is equal to or larger than the denominator (e.g. 7/4)
- **Mixed number** -- a whole number combined with a proper fraction (e.g. 1 3/4)

### Converting Between Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers

**Improper to mixed:** Divide numerator by denominator. The quotient is the whole
number, the remainder is the new numerator.

    7/4 --> 7 / 4 = 1 remainder 3 --> 1 3/4

**Mixed to improper:** Multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator.

    2 3/5 --> (2 * 5 + 3) / 5 = 13/5

## Equivalent Fractions

Two fractions are equivalent if they represent the same value. You create equivalent
fractions by multiplying or dividing both the numerator and denominator by the same
nonzero number.

    1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 50/100

## Simplifying (Reducing) Fractions

To simplify a fraction, divide both numerator and denominator by their greatest
common divisor (GCD).

    12/18 --> GCD of 12 and 18 is 6 --> 12/18 = 2/3

**Finding the GCD:** List the factors of each number, or use repeated division.

    Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
    Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
    Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6
    GCD = 6

## Comparing Fractions

To compare fractions with different denominators, find a common denominator, then
compare numerators.

    Which is larger, 3/5 or 2/3?

    3/5 = 9/15
    2/3 = 10/15

    10/15 > 9/15, so 2/3 > 3/5

**Cross multiplication shortcut:** Compare a/b and c/d by comparing a*d and b*c.

    3 * 3 = 9
    5 * 2 = 10

    Since 9 < 10, we get 3/5 < 2/3

## Operations with Fractions

### Addition and Subtraction

Fractions must have the same denominator before you can add or subtract them.

**Same denominator:** Add (or subtract) the numerators, keep the denominator.

    2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7
    5/8 - 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2

**Different denominators:** Find the least common denominator (LCD), convert, then operate.

    1/3 + 1/4
    LCD of 3 and 4 = 12
    1/3 = 4/12
    1/4 = 3/12
    4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

**Finding the LCD:** The LCD is the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators.
For small numbers, list multiples:

    Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15 ...
    Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16 ...
    LCM = 12

### Multiplication

Multiply numerator by numerator, denominator by denominator. No common denominator
needed.

    2/3 * 4/5 = 8/15

**Tip:** Simplify before multiplying (cross-cancel) to keep numbers small.

    3/4 * 2/9
    The 3 in the numerator and 9 in the denominator share a factor of 3:
    1/4 * 2/3 = 2/12 = 1/6

### Division

To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (flip the second fraction).

    3/4 / 2/5 = 3/4 * 5/2 = 15/8 = 1 7/8

### Operations with Mixed Numbers

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first, perform the operation, then
convert back if desired.

    2 1/3 + 1 1/2
    = 7/3 + 3/2
    = 14/6 + 9/6
    = 23/6
    = 3 5/6

## Fractions and Decimals

**Fraction to decimal:** Divide the numerator by the denominator.

    3/8 = 3 / 8 = 0.375

**Decimal to fraction:** Place the decimal over the appropriate power of 10 and simplify.

    0.75 = 75/100 = 3/4

### Common Equivalences Worth Memorizing

| Fraction | Decimal | Percentage |
|----------|---------|------------|
| 1/2      | 0.5     | 50%        |
| 1/3      | 0.333...| 33.3%      |
| 2/3      | 0.666...| 66.7%      |
| 1/4      | 0.25    | 25%        |
| 3/4      | 0.75    | 75%        |
| 1/5      | 0.2     | 20%        |
| 1/8      | 0.125   | 12.5%      |
| 1/10     | 0.1     | 10%        |

## Order of Operations with Fractions

The standard order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) applies to fractions just as it
does to whole numbers:

    1/2 + 1/3 * 3/4
    = 1/2 + (1/3 * 3/4)     -- multiplication first
    = 1/2 + 1/4
    = 2/4 + 1/4
    = 3/4

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Adding denominators:** 1/3 + 1/4 is NOT 2/7. You need a common denominator.
- **Forgetting to simplify:** Always check if your answer can be reduced.
- **Flipping the wrong fraction** when dividing: Flip the divisor (the second fraction), not the first.
- **Ignoring negative signs:** -3/4 and 3/(-4) are both equal to -(3/4). Keep track of signs.
