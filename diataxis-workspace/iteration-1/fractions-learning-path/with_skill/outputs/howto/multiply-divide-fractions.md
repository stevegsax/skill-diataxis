# How to Multiply and Divide Fractions

This guide shows you how to multiply and divide fractions in practical scenarios.

## How to multiply fractions and simplify the result

**Goal**: Compute the product of two fractions and express it in lowest terms.

1. Multiply the numerators together.
    - 2/3 * 4/5: numerator = 2 * 4 = 8

2. Multiply the denominators together.
    - denominator = 3 * 5 = 15

3. Write the result as a single fraction.
    - 8/15

4. Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by their GCF.
    - GCF(8, 15) = 1, so 8/15 is already simplified.

**Result**: 2/3 * 4/5 = 8/15

**Efficiency tip**: Cross-cancel before multiplying. If any numerator shares a
common factor with any denominator, divide both by that factor first. This keeps
the numbers smaller and often eliminates the simplification step.

Example: 4/9 * 3/8

- 4 and 8 share factor 4: replace 4 with 1, 8 with 2
- 3 and 9 share factor 3: replace 3 with 1, 9 with 3
- Now multiply: 1/3 * 1/2 = 1/6

## How to divide fractions

**Goal**: Compute one fraction divided by another.

1. Write down the first fraction (the dividend).
    - 2/3

2. Take the reciprocal of the second fraction (the divisor).
    - Reciprocal of 4/5 is 5/4.

3. Multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal.
    - 2/3 * 5/4 = 10/12

4. Simplify.
    - GCF(10, 12) = 2. Result: 5/6.

**Result**: 2/3 / 4/5 = 5/6

If dividing by a whole number, write it as a fraction first (e.g., 2 = 2/1),
then take its reciprocal (1/2) and multiply.

## How to scale a recipe using fraction multiplication

**Goal**: Adjust recipe quantities by a fractional factor.

Suppose a recipe calls for 2/3 cup of sugar and you want to make 1 1/2 times
the recipe.

1. Convert the scaling factor to an improper fraction.
    - 1 1/2 = 3/2

2. Multiply each ingredient quantity by the scaling factor.
    - 2/3 * 3/2

3. Cross-cancel if possible.
    - 3 appears in both a numerator and a denominator: cancel to get 2/1 * 1/2

4. Multiply.
    - 2/2 = 1

**Result**: You need 1 cup of sugar for 1.5x the recipe.

Repeat step 2 for each ingredient in the recipe.
