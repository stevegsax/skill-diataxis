# Tutorial: Adding and Subtracting Fractions

In this tutorial, we will learn to add and subtract fractions step by step. We
will start with the simplest case --- same denominators --- and work our way up
to mixed numbers with different denominators. By the end, you will be able to
combine any two fractions confidently.

We will use a cooking context throughout: imagine you are combining measured
ingredients.

## What we will need

- Comfort with fraction fundamentals (numerator, denominator, equivalent
  fractions, simplifying). If you need a refresher, start with the
  [Fraction Fundamentals Tutorial](fraction-fundamentals.md).

## Step 1: Adding fractions with the same denominator

Suppose you pour 1/4 cup of oil into a bowl, then add another 2/4 cup.

The denominators are the same (both fourths), so we simply add the numerators:

```
1/4 + 2/4 = (1 + 2)/4 = 3/4
```

You should see that we kept the denominator at 4 and only added the tops:
1 plus 2 equals 3, so the answer is 3/4.

**Result**: 3/4 cup of oil in the bowl.

Let's do a second example. You measure 3/8 cup of vinegar and add 2/8 cup more:

```
3/8 + 2/8 = (3 + 2)/8 = 5/8
```

Again, the denominators match (both eighths), so we add numerators: 3 plus 2
equals 5. The answer is 5/8 cup of vinegar.

## Step 2: Why different denominators are a problem

Now suppose we need 1/3 cup of water and 1/4 cup of lemon juice. How much
liquid is that in total?

We cannot write 1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7. That would be wrong --- we would be adding
pieces of different sizes. A third of a cup is bigger than a quarter of a cup,
so the pieces are not interchangeable.

The rule is: **we can only add fractions when the pieces are the same size**,
meaning the denominators must match. We need a common denominator.

## Step 3: Finding the Least Common Denominator (LCD)

To add 1/3 + 1/4, we need a denominator that both 3 and 4 divide into evenly.

List multiples:

- Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, **12**, 15, 18...
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**, 16, 20...

The smallest shared multiple is **12**. That is our LCD.

Let's find one more LCD for practice. Suppose we need the LCD of 6 and 8:

- Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, **24**, 30...
- Multiples of 8: 8, 16, **24**, 32...

The LCD of 6 and 8 is **24**.

## Step 4: Converting and adding

Now let's finish our problem: 1/3 + 1/4 with LCD = 12.

Convert each fraction to twelfths. We need to multiply each fraction's top and
bottom by the number that makes the denominator equal 12:

```
1/3 = ?/12    -->   3 x 4 = 12, so multiply top by 4:  1 x 4 = 4   -->   4/12
1/4 = ?/12    -->   4 x 3 = 12, so multiply top by 3:  1 x 3 = 3   -->   3/12
```

Now add:

```
4/12 + 3/12 = (4 + 3)/12 = 7/12
```

**Result**: 1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12 cup of liquid.

Let's do a second example: 2/5 + 1/4.

1. Find the LCD of 5 and 4:
    - Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, **20**, 25...
    - Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, **20**...
    - LCD = **20**

2. Convert:
    - 2/5: multiply top and bottom by 4: (2 x 4)/(5 x 4) = **8/20**
    - 1/4: multiply top and bottom by 5: (1 x 5)/(4 x 5) = **5/20**

3. Add: 8/20 + 5/20 = (8 + 5)/20 = **13/20**

**Result**: 2/5 + 1/4 = 13/20.

## Step 5: Subtracting fractions

Subtraction follows the exact same process. Suppose you have 3/4 cup of flour
and use 1/3 cup. How much is left?

1. Find the LCD of 4 and 3: **12**
2. Convert:
    - 3/4: multiply top and bottom by 3: (3 x 3)/(4 x 3) = **9/12**
    - 1/3: multiply top and bottom by 4: (1 x 4)/(3 x 4) = **4/12**
3. Subtract the numerators: 9 - 4 = 5
    - 9/12 - 4/12 = **5/12**

**Result**: 3/4 - 1/3 = 5/12 cup of flour remains.

Let's try another: 5/6 - 1/4.

1. Find the LCD of 6 and 4:
    - Multiples of 6: 6, **12**, 18...
    - Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**, 16...
    - LCD = **12**
2. Convert:
    - 5/6: multiply top and bottom by 2: (5 x 2)/(6 x 2) = **10/12**
    - 1/4: multiply top and bottom by 3: (1 x 3)/(4 x 3) = **3/12**
3. Subtract: 10/12 - 3/12 = (10 - 3)/12 = **7/12**

**Result**: 5/6 - 1/4 = 7/12.

## Step 6: Adding mixed numbers

You have 2 1/2 cups of broth and add 1 2/3 cups more. What is the total?

**Method**: Convert to improper fractions, then add.

1. Convert each mixed number to an improper fraction:
    - 2 1/2: multiply 2 x 2 = 4, add 1 = 5, keep denominator 2: **5/2**
    - 1 2/3: multiply 1 x 3 = 3, add 2 = 5, keep denominator 3: **5/3**

2. Find the LCD of 2 and 3: **6**

3. Convert to sixths:
    - 5/2: multiply top and bottom by 3: (5 x 3)/(2 x 3) = **15/6**
    - 5/3: multiply top and bottom by 2: (5 x 2)/(3 x 2) = **10/6**

4. Add: 15/6 + 10/6 = (15 + 10)/6 = **25/6**

5. Convert back to a mixed number:
    - 25 / 6 = 4 remainder 1
    - **25/6 = 4 1/6**

**Result**: 2 1/2 + 1 2/3 = 4 1/6 cups of broth.

Let's do another: 3 3/4 + 2 1/5.

1. Convert to improper fractions:
    - 3 3/4: multiply 3 x 4 = 12, add 3 = 15, denominator 4: **15/4**
    - 2 1/5: multiply 2 x 5 = 10, add 1 = 11, denominator 5: **11/5**

2. Find the LCD of 4 and 5: **20**

3. Convert to twentieths:
    - 15/4: multiply top and bottom by 5: (15 x 5)/(4 x 5) = **75/20**
    - 11/5: multiply top and bottom by 4: (11 x 4)/(5 x 4) = **44/20**

4. Add: 75/20 + 44/20 = (75 + 44)/20 = **119/20**

5. Convert back:
    - 119 / 20 = 5 remainder 19
    - **119/20 = 5 19/20**

**Result**: 3 3/4 + 2 1/5 = 5 19/20.

## Step 7: Subtracting mixed numbers

You have 4 1/3 cups of sugar and use 1 3/4 cups. How much is left?

1. Convert to improper fractions:
    - 4 1/3: multiply 4 x 3 = 12, add 1 = 13, denominator 3: **13/3**
    - 1 3/4: multiply 1 x 4 = 4, add 3 = 7, denominator 4: **7/4**

2. Find the LCD of 3 and 4: **12**

3. Convert to twelfths:
    - 13/3: multiply top and bottom by 4: (13 x 4)/(3 x 4) = **52/12**
    - 7/4: multiply top and bottom by 3: (7 x 3)/(4 x 3) = **21/12**

4. Subtract: 52/12 - 21/12 = (52 - 21)/12 = **31/12**

5. Convert back:
    - 31 / 12 = 2 remainder 7
    - **31/12 = 2 7/12**

**Result**: 4 1/3 - 1 3/4 = 2 7/12 cups of sugar remaining.

Let's try one more: 5 1/2 - 2 2/3.

1. Convert to improper fractions:
    - 5 1/2: multiply 5 x 2 = 10, add 1 = 11, denominator 2: **11/2**
    - 2 2/3: multiply 2 x 3 = 6, add 2 = 8, denominator 3: **8/3**

2. Find the LCD of 2 and 3: **6**

3. Convert to sixths:
    - 11/2: multiply top and bottom by 3: (11 x 3)/(2 x 3) = **33/6**
    - 8/3: multiply top and bottom by 2: (8 x 2)/(3 x 2) = **16/6**

4. Subtract: 33/6 - 16/6 = (33 - 16)/6 = **17/6**

5. Convert back:
    - 17 / 6 = 2 remainder 5
    - **17/6 = 2 5/6**

**Result**: 5 1/2 - 2 2/3 = 2 5/6.

## What we accomplished

In this tutorial, we:

- Added fractions with the same denominator (1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4)
- Found the LCD to add fractions with different denominators
- Added fractions with different denominators (1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12, 2/5 + 1/4 = 13/20)
- Subtracted fractions using the same LCD method (3/4 - 1/3 = 5/12, 5/6 - 1/4 = 7/12)
- Added mixed numbers (2 1/2 + 1 2/3 = 4 1/6, 3 3/4 + 2 1/5 = 5 19/20)
- Subtracted mixed numbers (4 1/3 - 1 3/4 = 2 7/12, 5 1/2 - 2 2/3 = 2 5/6)
