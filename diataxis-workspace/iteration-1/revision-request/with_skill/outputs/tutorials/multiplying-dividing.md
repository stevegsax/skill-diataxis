# Tutorial: Multiplying and Dividing Fractions

In this tutorial, we will learn to multiply and divide fractions. The good news:
multiplication is actually simpler than addition (no common denominators needed).
Division has one extra step but builds directly on multiplication. By the end,
you will be able to handle both operations with fractions and mixed numbers.

We will use recipe scaling as our running example.

## What we will need

- Comfort with fraction fundamentals and simplifying. If needed, review the
  [Fraction Fundamentals Tutorial](fraction-fundamentals.md).

## Step 1: Multiplying two fractions

A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of rice. You want to make half the recipe. How much
rice do you need?

"Half of 2/3" means 1/2 x 2/3.

To multiply fractions: **multiply the numerators, multiply the denominators**.

```
1/2 x 2/3 = (1 x 2) / (2 x 3) = 2/6
```

The numerators: 1 times 2 equals 2. The denominators: 2 times 3 equals 6.

Now simplify: GCF of 2 and 6 is 2. Divide both by 2:

```
2/6 = (2 / 2) / (6 / 2) = 1/3
```

**Result**: You need 1/3 cup of rice. Notice that multiplying by 1/2 gave us a
*smaller* result --- half of 2/3 is 1/3. This makes sense: taking a fraction of
a fraction gives a smaller piece.

Let's try a second example: 3/4 x 2/5.

```
3/4 x 2/5 = (3 x 2) / (4 x 5) = 6/20
```

Numerators: 3 times 2 equals 6. Denominators: 4 times 5 equals 20.

Simplify: GCF of 6 and 20 is 2. Divide both by 2:

```
6/20 = (6 / 2) / (20 / 2) = 3/10
```

**Result**: 3/4 x 2/5 = 3/10.

## Step 2: Multiplying a fraction by a whole number

The recipe calls for 3/4 teaspoon of salt. You want to triple it.

Write 3 as a fraction: 3/1. Then multiply:

```
3/1 x 3/4 = (3 x 3) / (1 x 4) = 9/4
```

Numerators: 3 times 3 equals 9. Denominators: 1 times 4 equals 4.

Convert to a mixed number: 9 / 4 = 2 remainder 1, so 9/4 = 2 1/4.

**Result**: You need 2 1/4 teaspoons of salt.

Another example: you need 2/5 cup of honey and want to make 4 batches.

```
4/1 x 2/5 = (4 x 2) / (1 x 5) = 8/5
```

Numerators: 4 times 2 equals 8. Denominators: 1 times 5 equals 5.

Convert: 8 / 5 = 1 remainder 3, so 8/5 = 1 3/5.

**Result**: You need 1 3/5 cups of honey for 4 batches.

## Step 3: Understanding reciprocals

Before we can divide, we need to understand reciprocals.

The **reciprocal** of a fraction is that fraction flipped upside down:

```
Reciprocal of 3/4  =  4/3
Reciprocal of 2/5  =  5/2
Reciprocal of 7/1  =  1/7
Reciprocal of 1/6  =  6/1  =  6
```

A key property: any number multiplied by its reciprocal equals 1. Let's verify
with 3/4:

```
3/4 x 4/3 = (3 x 4) / (4 x 3) = 12/12 = 1
```

Numerators: 3 times 4 equals 12. Denominators: 4 times 3 equals 12. And 12/12
simplifies to 1. The 3s and 4s appear in both positions, so everything cancels.

## Step 4: Dividing fractions

You have 3/4 cup of sugar and want to split it equally into 2 containers. How
much goes in each?

This is 3/4 / 2.

The rule for dividing fractions: **multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor**.

1. Write 2 as a fraction: 2/1
2. Find its reciprocal: 1/2
3. Multiply: 3/4 x 1/2 = (3 x 1) / (4 x 2) = 3/8

The numerators: 3 times 1 equals 3. The denominators: 4 times 2 equals 8.

**Result**: Each container gets 3/8 cup of sugar.

Let's try fraction-by-fraction: 2/3 / 4/5.

1. Reciprocal of 4/5 is 5/4
2. Multiply: 2/3 x 5/4 = (2 x 5) / (3 x 4) = 10/12
3. Simplify: GCF of 10 and 12 is 2. Divide both by 2: 10/12 = 5/6

**Result**: 2/3 / 4/5 = 5/6.

Another example: 5/8 / 3/4.

1. Reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3
2. Multiply: 5/8 x 4/3 = (5 x 4) / (8 x 3) = 20/24
3. Simplify: GCF of 20 and 24 is 4. Divide both by 4: 20/24 = 5/6

**Result**: 5/8 / 3/4 = 5/6.

## Step 5: Multiplying mixed numbers

A recipe serves 4 people and calls for 1 1/2 cups of flour. You want to serve 6
(that is 1 1/2 times the recipe).

The rule for mixed numbers: **convert to improper fractions first**, then multiply.

1. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions:
    - 1 1/2: multiply 1 x 2 = 2, add 1 = 3, denominator 2: **3/2**
    - 1 1/2: same conversion: **3/2**

2. Multiply:
    - 3/2 x 3/2 = (3 x 3) / (2 x 2) = 9/4

3. Convert back to a mixed number:
    - 9 / 4 = 2 remainder 1
    - **9/4 = 2 1/4**

**Result**: You need 2 1/4 cups of flour.

Another example: 2 1/3 x 1 1/4.

1. Convert:
    - 2 1/3: multiply 2 x 3 = 6, add 1 = 7, denominator 3: **7/3**
    - 1 1/4: multiply 1 x 4 = 4, add 1 = 5, denominator 4: **5/4**

2. Multiply: 7/3 x 5/4 = (7 x 5) / (3 x 4) = **35/12**

3. Convert back: 35 / 12 = 2 remainder 11, so **35/12 = 2 11/12**

**Result**: 2 1/3 x 1 1/4 = 2 11/12.

## Step 6: Dividing mixed numbers

You have 3 3/4 cups of batter and want to divide it equally among 2 1/2 pans.
How much goes in each pan?

This is 3 3/4 / 2 1/2.

1. Convert to improper fractions:
    - 3 3/4: multiply 3 x 4 = 12, add 3 = 15, denominator 4: **15/4**
    - 2 1/2: multiply 2 x 2 = 4, add 1 = 5, denominator 2: **5/2**

2. Take the reciprocal of the divisor: reciprocal of 5/2 is **2/5**

3. Multiply: 15/4 x 2/5 = (15 x 2) / (4 x 5) = 30/20

4. Simplify: GCF of 30 and 20 is 10. Divide both by 10: 30/20 = **3/2**

5. Convert back: 3/2 = **1 1/2**

**Result**: 3 3/4 / 2 1/2 = 1 1/2 cups per pan.

## What we accomplished

In this tutorial, we:

- Multiplied fractions by multiplying numerators and denominators straight across
  (1/2 x 2/3 = 1/3, 3/4 x 2/5 = 3/10)
- Multiplied fractions by whole numbers (3 x 3/4 = 9/4 = 2 1/4)
- Learned what reciprocals are and verified that a number times its reciprocal equals 1
- Divided fractions using "invert and multiply" (2/3 / 4/5 = 5/6, 5/8 / 3/4 = 5/6)
- Multiplied mixed numbers by converting to improper fractions first
  (2 1/3 x 1 1/4 = 2 11/12)
- Divided mixed numbers (3 3/4 / 2 1/2 = 1 1/2)
