# Tutorial: Multiplying and Dividing Fractions

In this tutorial, we will learn to multiply and divide fractions. The good news:
multiplication is actually simpler than addition (no common denominators needed).
Division has one extra step but builds directly on multiplication. By the end,
you will be able to handle both operations with fractions and mixed numbers.

We will use recipe scaling as our running example.

## What we will need

- Comfort with fraction fundamentals and simplifying. If needed, review the
  [Fraction Fundamentals Tutorial](fraction-fundamentals.md).
- Familiarity with mixed numbers and improper fractions. If needed, review the
  [Understanding Mixed Numbers Tutorial](mixed-numbers.md).

## Step 1: Multiplying two fractions

A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of rice. You want to make half the recipe. How much
rice do you need?

"Half of 2/3" means 1/2 x 2/3.

To multiply fractions: **multiply the numerators, multiply the denominators**.

```
1/2 x 2/3 = (1 x 2) / (2 x 3) = 2/6
```

Now simplify: GCF of 2 and 6 is 2.

```
2/6 = 1/3
```

**Result**: You need 1/3 cup of rice. Notice that multiplying by 1/2 gave us a
*smaller* result --- half of 2/3 is 1/3. This makes sense: taking a fraction of
a fraction gives a smaller piece.

**Another example**: A garden is 3/4 of an acre. You plant tomatoes on 2/5 of the
garden. How much of the total acre is tomatoes?

```
3/4 x 2/5 = (3 x 2) / (4 x 5) = 6/20
```

Simplify: GCF of 6 and 20 is 2.

```
6/20 = 3/10
```

**Result**: Tomatoes cover **3/10** of an acre.

## Step 2: Multiplying a fraction by a whole number

The recipe calls for 3/4 teaspoon of salt. You want to triple it (make 3 batches).

Write 3 as a fraction: 3/1. Then multiply:

```
3/1 x 3/4 = (3 x 3) / (1 x 4) = 9/4
```

Convert to a mixed number: 9/4 = 2 1/4.

**Result**: You need 2 1/4 teaspoons of salt.

**Another example**: You walk 2/5 of a mile each day. How far do you walk in 7
days?

```
7/1 x 2/5 = (7 x 2) / (1 x 5) = 14/5
```

Convert: 14/5 = 2 4/5.

**Result**: You walk **2 4/5** miles in a week.

## Step 3: Understanding reciprocals

Before we can divide, we need to understand reciprocals.

The **reciprocal** of a fraction is that fraction flipped upside down:

```
Reciprocal of 3/4  =  4/3
Reciprocal of 2/5  =  5/2
Reciprocal of 7/1  =  1/7
```

A key property: any number multiplied by its reciprocal equals 1.

```
3/4 x 4/3 = 12/12 = 1
2/5 x 5/2 = 10/10 = 1
```

You should see that the numbers appear in both positions, so everything cancels
to 1.

## Step 4: Dividing fractions

You have 3/4 cup of sugar and want to split it equally into 2 containers. How
much goes in each?

This is 3/4 / 2.

The rule for dividing fractions: **multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor**.

1. Write 2 as a fraction: 2/1
2. Find its reciprocal: 1/2
3. Multiply: 3/4 x 1/2 = 3/8

**Result**: Each container gets **3/8** cup of sugar.

**Another example**: You have 2/3 of a pound of trail mix and want to divide it
into bags that each hold 1/6 of a pound. How many bags can you fill?

This is 2/3 / 1/6.

1. Reciprocal of 1/6 is 6/1
2. Multiply: 2/3 x 6/1 = 12/3
3. Simplify: 12/3 = 4

**Result**: You can fill **4** bags.

**One more**: 4/5 / 2/3.

1. Reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2
2. Multiply: 4/5 x 3/2 = 12/10
3. Simplify: GCF(12, 10) = 2, so 12/10 = 6/5 = 1 1/5

**Result**: 4/5 / 2/3 = **1 1/5**.

## Step 5: Multiplying mixed numbers

A recipe serves 4 people and calls for 1 1/2 cups of flour. You want to serve 6
(that is 1 1/2 times the recipe).

1. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions:
    - 1 1/2 = 3/2 (the amount of flour)
    - 1 1/2 = 3/2 (the scaling factor)

2. Multiply:
    - 3/2 x 3/2 = 9/4

3. Convert back:
    - 9/4 = 2 1/4

**Result**: You need **2 1/4** cups of flour.

**Another example**: A room is 3 1/3 yards long and 2 1/4 yards wide. What is the
area?

1. Convert: 3 1/3 = 10/3, 2 1/4 = 9/4
2. Multiply: 10/3 x 9/4 = 90/12
3. Simplify: GCF(90, 12) = 6, so 90/12 = 15/2
4. Convert: 15/2 = 7 1/2

**Result**: The area is **7 1/2** square yards.

## Step 6: Dividing mixed numbers

You have 3 3/4 cups of juice and want to pour equal amounts into glasses that
each hold 5/8 of a cup. How many glasses can you fill?

1. Convert: 3 3/4 = 15/4
2. Divide: 15/4 / 5/8
3. Reciprocal of 5/8 is 8/5
4. Multiply: 15/4 x 8/5 = 120/20
5. Simplify: 120/20 = 6

**Result**: You can fill **6** glasses.

**Another example**: A rope is 5 1/4 feet long. You cut it into pieces that are
each 1 3/4 feet long. How many pieces do you get?

1. Convert: 5 1/4 = 21/4, 1 3/4 = 7/4
2. Divide: 21/4 / 7/4
3. Reciprocal of 7/4 is 4/7
4. Multiply: 21/4 x 4/7 = 84/28
5. Simplify: 84/28 = 3

**Result**: You get **3** pieces of rope.

## What we accomplished

In this tutorial, we:

- Multiplied fractions by multiplying numerators and denominators straight across
- Multiplied fractions by whole numbers
- Learned what reciprocals are and why they matter
- Divided fractions using "invert and multiply"
- Multiplied and divided mixed numbers by converting to improper fractions first
