# Tutorial: Adding and Subtracting Fractions

In this tutorial, we will learn to add and subtract fractions step by step. We
will start with the simplest case --- same denominators --- and work our way up
to mixed numbers with different denominators. By the end, you will be able to
combine any two fractions confidently.

We will use a cooking context throughout: imagine you are combining measured
ingredients.

## What we will need

- Comfort with fraction fundamentals (numerator, denominator, equivalent
  fractions, simplifying). If you need a refresher, start with the
  [Fraction Fundamentals Tutorial](fraction-fundamentals.md).
- Familiarity with mixed numbers and improper fractions. If needed, review the
  [Understanding Mixed Numbers Tutorial](mixed-numbers.md).

## Step 1: Adding fractions with the same denominator

Suppose you pour 1/4 cup of olive oil into a bowl, then add another 2/4 cup.

The denominators are the same (both fourths), so we simply add the numerators:

```
1/4 + 2/4 = (1 + 2)/4 = 3/4
```

You should see that we kept the denominator at 4 and only added the tops.

**Result**: 3/4 cup of olive oil in the bowl.

**Another example**: You drink 3/8 of a gallon of water in the morning and 2/8 in
the afternoon.

```
3/8 + 2/8 = (3 + 2)/8 = 5/8
```

**Result**: You drank 5/8 of a gallon total.

## Step 2: Subtracting fractions with the same denominator

You have 5/6 of a pound of butter and use 2/6 of a pound for a recipe.

```
5/6 - 2/6 = (5 - 2)/6 = 3/6
```

Now simplify: GCF of 3 and 6 is 3.

```
3/6 = 1/2
```

**Result**: You have 1/2 pound of butter left.

## Step 3: Why different denominators are a problem

Now suppose we need 1/3 cup of water and 1/4 cup of lemon juice. How much
liquid is that in total?

We cannot write 1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7. That would be wrong --- we would be adding
pieces of different sizes. Think of it this way: one-third of a cup is a larger
piece than one-quarter of a cup, so the answer must be more than 1/2. But 2/7 is
less than 1/2. Something is clearly wrong.

The rule is: **we can only add fractions when the pieces are the same size**,
meaning the denominators must match. We need a common denominator.

## Step 4: Finding the Least Common Denominator (LCD)

To add 1/3 + 1/4, we need a denominator that both 3 and 4 divide into evenly.

List multiples:

- Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, **12**, 15...
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**, 16...

The smallest shared multiple is **12**. That is our LCD.

**Another example**: Find the LCD for 2/5 + 1/6.

- Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, **30**...
- Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, **30**...

The LCD is **30**.

## Step 5: Converting and adding with different denominators

Now convert each fraction to twelfths and add:

```
1/3 = ?/12    -->   1 x 4 = 4,  3 x 4 = 12   -->   4/12
1/4 = ?/12    -->   1 x 3 = 3,  4 x 3 = 12   -->   3/12
```

Now add:

```
4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12
```

**Result**: 1/3 + 1/4 = **7/12** cup of liquid.

**Let's work through the second example**: 2/5 + 1/6, with LCD = 30.

```
2/5 = ?/30    -->   2 x 6 = 12,  5 x 6 = 30   -->   12/30
1/6 = ?/30    -->   1 x 5 = 5,   6 x 5 = 30   -->   5/30
```

Add:

```
12/30 + 5/30 = 17/30
```

**Result**: 2/5 + 1/6 = **17/30**.

## Step 6: Subtracting fractions with different denominators

Subtraction follows the exact same process. Suppose you have 3/4 cup of flour
and use 1/3 cup. How much is left?

1. Find the LCD of 4 and 3: **12**
2. Convert:
    - 3/4 = 9/12
    - 1/3 = 4/12
3. Subtract:
    - 9/12 - 4/12 = 5/12

**Result**: 5/12 cup of flour remains.

**Another example**: You ran 5/6 of a mile yesterday and 1/4 of a mile today. How
much farther did you run yesterday?

1. LCD of 6 and 4: multiples of 6 are 6, **12**...; multiples of 4 are 4, 8, **12**... LCD = **12**
2. Convert: 5/6 = 10/12, 1/4 = 3/12
3. Subtract: 10/12 - 3/12 = 7/12

**Result**: You ran **7/12** of a mile farther yesterday.

## Step 7: Adding mixed numbers

You have 2 1/2 cups of broth and add 1 2/3 cups more. What is the total?

**Method**: Convert to improper fractions, then add.

1. Convert:
    - 2 1/2 = (2 x 2 + 1)/2 = 5/2
    - 1 2/3 = (1 x 3 + 2)/3 = 5/3

2. Find the LCD of 2 and 3: **6**

3. Convert:
    - 5/2 = 15/6
    - 5/3 = 10/6

4. Add:
    - 15/6 + 10/6 = 25/6

5. Convert back to a mixed number:
    - 25 / 6 = 4 remainder 1
    - 25/6 = 4 1/6

**Result**: 2 1/2 + 1 2/3 = **4 1/6** cups of broth.

**Another example**: A board is 3 3/4 feet long. You glue on a piece that is
1 2/3 feet long. What is the total length?

1. Convert: 3 3/4 = 15/4, 1 2/3 = 5/3
2. LCD of 4 and 3: **12**
3. Convert: 15/4 = 45/12, 5/3 = 20/12
4. Add: 45/12 + 20/12 = 65/12
5. Convert back: 65 / 12 = 5 remainder 5, so 65/12 = 5 5/12

**Result**: The total board length is **5 5/12** feet.

## Step 8: Subtracting mixed numbers

You have 4 1/3 pounds of ground beef and use 1 3/4 pounds for burgers. How much
is left?

1. Convert: 4 1/3 = 13/3, 1 3/4 = 7/4
2. LCD of 3 and 4: **12**
3. Convert: 13/3 = 52/12, 7/4 = 21/12
4. Subtract: 52/12 - 21/12 = 31/12
5. Convert back: 31 / 12 = 2 remainder 7, so 31/12 = 2 7/12

**Result**: You have **2 7/12** pounds of ground beef left.

## What we accomplished

In this tutorial, we:

- Added and subtracted fractions with the same denominator by working with the numerators only
- Found the LCD to handle fractions with different denominators
- Added and subtracted fractions with different denominators using equivalent fractions
- Added and subtracted mixed numbers by converting to improper fractions first
